Ala Moana Center

The largest open-air shopping center in the world

Ala Moana Center, the largest shopping mall in Hawaii boasting over more than 290 stores, which includes 80 dining options 4 major department stores and several luxury retailers. There is a store for everything you need and want and more. Getting tired and hungry from all of the shopping? Head on down to the Makai Market food court to rest and fuel up for another lap around the 2.3 million square feet of retail goodness.

Kalakaua Avenue

The Heart of Waikiki

Typically speaking most people don't put a street as a destination to visit, however Kalakaua have more to offer than tar and gravel. Adjoined this 1 and a half mile strip are over 100 different shops and restaurant, several high end hotel, and the International Marketplace another shopping center. Not only are there shops and restaurant, on certain day there are parades. Along side Kalakaua Aveune is the Waikiki beach walk, for when you want to take a break and enjoy the beach and sun.

Aloha Tower Marketplace

Nestled in the Honolulu Harbor

the Aloha Tower Marketplace offers a wide variety of shopping, dining, and entertainment options, as well as a festive "Boat Days" atmosphere. Built in 1926, the Aloha Tower welcomes travelers arriving on Oahu. It is the most recognized building in the state, and second only to Diamond Head as Hawai'i's most famous landmark. Ride the elevator to the top of its ten stories for panoramic views of Honolulu. The Tower is open daily, and admission is free. Shop for gifts and souvenirs at the Marketplace's more than 75 stores and kiosks. Enjoy dining with an ocean view and come early in the evening for Happy Hour specials. Stay late and soak up the atmosphere at popular hangouts like the Bikini Cantina or the Gordon Biersch Brewery and Restaurant.
